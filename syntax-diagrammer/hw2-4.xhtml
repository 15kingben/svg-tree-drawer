We use semantics to get around a limitation in syntax for number names in English: the syntax phrase structure tree for number names does not correspond to the associative operations of mathematics. However, the semantics overcomes the syntactic limitation by providing the proper associations between the terms in the expression, and once the terms and their operators—the predications—are gathered up to the root (via the Semantic Compositionability Principle), then the mathematical operations are free to take place regardless of the underlying syntactic tree structure.
