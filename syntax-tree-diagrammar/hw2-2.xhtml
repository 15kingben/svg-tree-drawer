Problem 2: Spanish NPs I

  1. Yes, Spanish nouns obey the SHAC. The Specifier-Head Agreement Constraint requires that a head and its specifier must have the same agreement features. In Spanish, gender is an agreement feature, and both the noun (head) and its the article (specifier) must share the same gender (as well as number).
  2. No, the feature GEND(ER) is appropriate for all agreement categories in Spanish. This is because all nouns have gender, not just 3rd person singular personal pronouns, as in English.
